variables in a programming language are like variables in an algebra class.

if algebra wasn't your strong suit, then let me put it in layman's terms:

when you order your psl at Starbucks, you normally say the size, what you want in it and your name.
a variable is like a Starbucks cup that can have a size, put stuff into and have your name written onto.

the tall, in the coding example, is the size of our cup or variable.
in computer science terms, the size is called a type

the name, ana or tori, we give our cup must follow the size like when we order our tasty beverages.
in computer science terms, the name is called an identifier

the equals sign, in the code, is just saying: this is what i want inside my cup,
and anything after the = is what gets put inside of our cups.

so, right now, we have 2 beverages:
a tall for ana with 15 (let's say ml of coffee) inside of it
a tall for tori with 15 (let's say ml of coffee) inside of it

this section, in computer science terms, is called an if block

basically, when you want to do something, but want to make sure it only happens at a certain time, you want to use an if block

in layman's terms:


say, Starbucks wants to celebrate National Coffee Day (09-29) by having a bogo sale.

when ringing up your drinks at the register, the register would have to check the date, and if the date is 09-29, then the register gives the customer the bogo discount.

in that sentence, above, the if...then... would be considered an if block in a programmer's perspective!

in psl:


think of um as if and the : (colon) as then
duh would be the period at the end of the sentence.

in layman's terms:
everything between if and then, in computer science terms, is called a conditional. so, in our example above, the date is 09-29 would be our condition

in psl:
the condition would look something like #date like "09-29"

in layman's terms:
everything after the then would be what we'd want to happen if the condition is true. so, in our example, the register gives the customer the bogo discount would only happen if the date was 09-29 or National Coffee Day.

in psl:
this would look something like #subtotal = #subtotal / 2
